Abraham Lincoln

A man of Faith and Courage

The Timeline of Abraham Lincoln's Life

  • - Born in a one-room cabin in Hardin County, Kentucky.
  • - Ran for the Illinois General Assembly but lost. However, his ambition and drive remained undeterred.
  • - Elected to the Illinois State Legislature, where he began to make his mark on American politics.
  • - Married Mary Todd, forming a partnership that would support him through his political career.
  • - Served in the U.S. House of Representatives, advocating for the abolition of slavery in Washington, D.C.
  • - Patented a device to lift boats over shoals and obstructions, making him the only U.S. president to hold a patent.
  • - Opposed the Kansas-Nebraska Act, which allowed for the expansion of slavery, solidifying his stance as a label anti-slavery advocate.
  • - Gained national recognition through his debates with Stephen A. Douglas during the Illinois Senate race, despite losing the election.
  • - Elected the 16th President of the United States, steering the country through its greatest moral, constitutional, and political crisis.
  • - Issued the Emancipation Proclamation, declaring that all slaves in Confederate-held territory were free.
  • - Delivered the Gettysburg Address, redefining the purpose of the war and reinforcing the principles of liberty and equality.
  • - Witnessed the surrender of Confederate General Robert E. Lee, marking the end of the Civil War.
  • - Assassinated by John Wilkes Booth at Ford's Theatre, leaving a legacy of unity and freedom that would shape the future of the nation.
